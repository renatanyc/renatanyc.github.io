100 New Friends Challenge

Posted on 2017-06-23

I decided to start this challenge because I want to jump out of my comfort zone, improve communication skills and meet more interesting people to not only achieve career goals but also gain confidence.

I am a typical Chinese girl - wearing glasses, speaking in a low voice, and can hardly be noticed in a networking event. One big reason is that I grew up in a very introvert family. I never experienced the business world until I joined the Enacts Club and attended the National Conference in Beijing during my fresh year. I found it is so hard or even frightening for me to hand my business card to a stranger and introduce myself. I still remember the jealous feeling my captain gave out all of her pink and impressive business cards with her mirror name in it.

Though I became more aware of the importance of communication and networking, I didn’t make any effective actions to improve it. Yes, I attended every networking event, I went to pronunciation classes, and I sent thank you notes to everyone after event. But I didn’t make any profession remember me and I even didn’t have the courage to ask for a coffee chat in LinkedIn.

What made me change my mind and take the action? Last Monday, I went to Bryant park and had an awakening lunch conversation with Difei. We studied at the same universities for bachelor and master, and she had worked in New York for almost three years. She told me that even she had more than 110+ on TOEFL exam, she still felt so awkward to answer those simple questions like “How are you?” on the first month she arrived in New York. She only had four Chinese friend and only spoke English. She wrote down more than 12 different replies for one same question in daily life. She said she always live in uncomfortable zone - every time when she went to manager’s office, every time when she went to start conversation with a new client. I realized that it is the time I need to change. I have a lot values to offer, and communication should not be the obstacle to prevent me from landing my dream job.

Then, on Monday night I participated in a program that I have hesitated for a long time. In this program, I need to make a new friend every day and record the process and thinking. It had passed 8 days and I still remember my hardest first day - I walked along the river for 3 times and finally found the one.

I have made great progress in pass week and this challenge is the best decision I’ve made in my life. I’m going to write down each new friend that I made during this program and share lesson that I learned.

The beginning is always the hardest, but after that everything is easy.
